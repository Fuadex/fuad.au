## 3. ◆ THE FABLE LENS — where a stronger model actually matters (ranked)

**Principle:** Fable earns its cost on *interpretation* — reading something difficult (a lyric,
a painting, a note) and writing a faithful, non-obvious, well-made short text. Everything else
(folding decks, fetching Wikidata, computing stats, keyword search) is mechanical and must NOT
consume scarce Fable time. The three apps each have a generative backlog that only a strong
model does well; here they are, ranked by value-per-Fable-token and by "permanence" (the output
is baked into the site forever).

### ① Canvas artwork close-readings — HIGHEST leverage, do first
- **State:** 16 of 313 works have `art-about.js` reads (Info + Interpretation). All 23 **floored**
  works + the strongest **loved** ones deserve them.
- **Why Fable:** a good artwork read (composition, light, history, "where to stand") is exactly
  the difficult-interpretation task Fable is best at, and Wikidata/OMDb can't produce it. The 16
  existing ones (Great Wave read right-to-left, Impression Sunrise's tonal trick) show the bar.
- **Scope to spend Fable on now:** write reads for the **remaining 7 floored** + a first tranche
  of the top loved works (Vermeers, the Claudel/Morisot discovery thread, Zorn, the Belvedere
  Klimts/Schieles). ~30–60 works. Batch it. This is the single best use of remaining Fable time —
  permanent, unique, and it completes Canvas's "learning" half.

### ② Rotation lyric interpretations — proven Fable strength, deep backlog
- **State:** 52 tracks with Fable reads; **85 hard songs** remain in the gen-fable divergence
  queue (where Haiku/Sonnet/Opus disagree most = genuinely ambiguous lyrics), plus a **270-track
  promotion backlog** and non-English material.
- **Why Fable:** the head-to-head already showed Fable's edge on *hard* lyrics (the reads that
  name the arc / the mechanism, not just the theme). The divergence queue is literally sorted to
  the songs that most need a careful reader.
- **Scope to spend Fable on now:** the next **20–40 hard-queue songs** (assets ready in
  `.sptmp`: `blurb-fable-candidates.md`, `gen-fable.js`). Dual-tier where the song rewards it.

### ③ Culture film/show interpretations — untapped, high potential
- **State:** zero Fable content in Culture. The §8 plan proposes it but nothing's built.
- **Two Fable-worthy tracks:** (a) **"what it's about" reads for the canon films/shows** —
  the exact Rotation llm-about pattern ported to film (a dual-tier read per favourite); (b)
  the **notes-corpus** angle — 723 Polish reader notes await EN redrafts (translation is
  lower-Fable-value, more mechanical) BUT generating *fresh interpretive reads* of the top-rated
  films is high Fable value.
- **Scope to spend Fable on now:** reads for the top ~20 films/shows (the 9-10★ tier) — proves
  the feature and gives Culture its first interpretive layer.

### ④ Cross-app, stretch — thematic twins & the taste essay
- Fable could write the **connective tissue**: a short "your taste in one paragraph" essay for
  Canvas Portrait / Rotation Overview / Culture, or the "X rhymes with Y across mediums" reads
  for the hub join. Lower priority than ①–③ (needs the joins built first) but pure Fable value.

### What NOT to spend Fable on
Deck folding, Wikidata/Commons fetches, palette extraction, keyword search, stats, layout,
the mechanical translation of notes — all handled by scripts, Sonnet subagents, or weaker
models. Reserve Fable for interpretation.
